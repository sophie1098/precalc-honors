Using Math to Make Sense of our World: Pandemics, Viruses, and our Actions

Many events have led to this year being one for the books. For example, the Australian bushfires, wildfires in California, the “murder hornets” arrival in the U.S., and much more. But, the thing that has been the most difficult for people to adapt to is the COVID-19 pandemic. While people are busy blaming the year 2020 for the pandemic, very few are actually analyzing the causes and effects. We can look at different aspects of this global pandemic with math. However, it is important to know some background information first.
